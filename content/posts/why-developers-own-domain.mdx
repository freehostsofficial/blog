## Your Name, Your Space

In a world of social media platforms that rise and fall, a domain name is the one piece of internet real estate you actually own. Twitter can rebrand, LinkedIn can change their algorithm, GitHub can update their terms — but yourdomain.com is yours.

For developers, a personal domain is more than vanity. It's infrastructure.

## The Professional Case

Every hiring manager we've spoken to says the same thing: a candidate with a polished personal site on their own domain makes a stronger impression than one with a GitHub profile alone. It shows initiative, taste, and the ability to ship something end-to-end.

Your domain becomes your canonical online identity. It's the URL you put on your résumé, your conference talks, your open-source contributions. It's the one link that will still work in ten years.

## The Practical Benefits

Beyond branding, owning a domain unlocks practical benefits. Custom email addresses look more professional. You can point subdomains to different projects. You control your SEO destiny. And you can switch hosting providers without breaking any links.

## The Cost Argument

A `.dev` or `.com` domain costs $10-15 per year. That's less than one dollar per month. Hosting is free. Email forwarding is free. There is genuinely no financial barrier.

## Getting Started

1. **Choose a domain**: Your full name is ideal. If it's taken, try adding "dev" or "code" as a suffix.
2. **Register it**: Cloudflare Registrar, Porkbun, or Namecheap offer the best prices.
3. **Build something simple**: A single-page site with your name, a short bio, and links to your work.
4. **Host it for free**: Cloudflare Pages, Vercel, or Netlify — all covered in our hosting directory.
5. **Set up email forwarding**: Most registrars offer this for free.

The whole process takes less than an hour. Do it today.
